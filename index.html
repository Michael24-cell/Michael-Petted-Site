THE TEACHER

Where truth, imagination, and craft collide.

Michael Petted has been a dedicated acting teacher since 2009, when he began teaching full-time at the renowned Beverly Hills Playhouse, where he spent ten years shaping the craft of hundreds of actors.

For eight years, Michael also served as the Head Acting Instructor for the Hollywood Immersive program, working with actors from around the world including Australia, England, and Africa, helping them bridge the gap between training and professional work.

"Today, Michael teaches his own scene study and on-camera acting class at The Hudson Theatre in Los Angeles, where his passion for truth, artistry, and practical technique continues to inspire actors at every level—from complete beginners to seasoned professionals."

The Playhouse is known for its celebrated alumni, including George Clooney, Michelle Pfeiffer, Alec Baldwin, Jorge Garcia, and Monica Barbaro.

A safe space for dangerous work.

"You'll be challenged but always feel safe to take risks, explore deeply, and grow through the work."

The work is real. The risk is worth it. Because great acting isn't performed—it's lived.

THE ARTIST BEHIND THE TEACHER

Acting, directing, and music credits

Screen Credits

  • As an actor, Michael's work spans television, film, and stage.
  • Screen credits include FX's American Horror Story (opposite Jessica Lange).
  • Other credits: FOX's New Girl, the indie feature Mayor Cupcake (alongside Lea Thompson, Judd Nelson, and Zoey Deutch), Spade (opposite Jorge Garcia), and A Good Thing (with Tom Bower and Anne Gee Byrd).
  • He's appeared in recurring sketches on NBC's The Late Late Show with Thomas Lennon and in the award-winning short Beautiful Dead Things, now streaming on Amazon Prime.

Stage & Awards

  • He earned an Ovation Award nomination for Best Actor for Lone Star (opposite Don Swayze, produced by Patrick Swayze and Doris Roberts).
  • He also starred in the Ovation-nominated A Gift from Heaven alongside Beth Grant and Tara Buck.
  • Acclaimed stage productions include Eric Bogosian's Suburbia at The Skylight Theatre Company, Sam Shepard's True West, John Patrick Shanley's Italian American Reconciliation, and Robert Bolt's A Man for All Seasons alongside John Glover and directed by Howard Deutch.

Michael's work is rooted in one belief: that truth, imagination, and courage are the core of every great performance. His mission is to help actors discover not just how to act, but how to live truthfully in the work - turning craft into art, one scene at a time.
