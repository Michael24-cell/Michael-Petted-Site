THE TEACHER

Michael Petted has been a dedicated acting teacher since 2009, when he began teaching full-time at the renowned Beverly Hills Playhouse, where he spent ten years shaping the craft of hundreds of actors.

For eight years, Michael also served as the Head Acting Instructor for the Hollywood Immersive program, working with actors from around the world including Australia, England, and Africa, helping them bridge the gap between training and professional work.

"Today, Michael teaches his own scene study and on-camera acting class at The Hudson Theatre in Los Angeles, where his passion for truth, artistry, and practical technique continues to inspire actors at every level—from complete beginners to seasoned professionals."

The Playhouse is known for its celebrated alumni, including George Clooney, Michelle Pfeiffer, Alec Baldwin, Jorge Garcia, and Monica Barbaro.

THE CLASS

Scene Study & On-Camera Training

Focus & Technique

Michael's class is based on moment-to-moment work, using imagination and lived experience to create authentic, exciting performances.

The training also emphasizes the realities of a working career, ensuring actors bridge the gap between training and professional work.

"Train with truth. Create with courage."

The Foundation

  • Based on moment-to-moment work: A real person in a real place.
  • Using imagination and real-life experiences to fulfill the storytelling.
  • Have fun and be playful.

Individual Approach

  • Individual approach to each student.
  • If something already works for you, great—keep doing it.

Your Impulse is Gold

  • As storytellers, ask: How do I want to tell this story? What are my impulses?
  • Your uniqueness is your gold. Mine it.
  • Learn to listen to your impulses and act on them. The goal is growth.

Preparation & Potency

  • Find out what story is being told first, then bring your ideas.
  • Create moments for yourself. Get personal with your work.

Industry Reality

  • A professional class for students who desire to grow and get work.
  • Know your casting. What parts should you be going after? Don't waste time.
  • What is your "invitation to the party"?

Try things. Experiment. Play.

Student Success

Students have gone on to work on acclaimed series and films including Yellowstone. Other credits include The Promised Land (current series lead), And Just Like That, A Big Bold Beautiful Journey, American Horror Story, and Far and Away (Australia), Little Voice, We Are Who We Are, How I Met Your Father, Forever (Lifetime Movie), 911, The Offer, among many others.

THE ARTIST BEHIND THE TEACHER

Acting, directing, and music credits

Screen Credits

  • As an actor, Michael's work spans television, film, and stage.
  • Screen credits include FX's American Horror Story (opposite Jessica Lange).
  • Other credits: FOX's New Girl, the indie feature Mayor Cupcake (alongside Lea Thompson, Judd Nelson, and Zoey Deutch), Spade (opposite Jorge Garcia), and A Good Thing (with Tom Bower and Anne Gee Byrd).
  • He's appeared in recurring sketches on NBC's The Late Late Show with Thomas Lennon and in the award-winning short Beautiful Dead Things, now streaming on Amazon Prime.

Stage & Awards

  • He earned a Los Angeles Ovation Award nomination for Best Actor for Lone Star (opposite Don Swayze, produced by Patrick Swayze and Doris Roberts).
  • He also starred in the Ovation-nominated A Gift from Heaven alongside Beth Grant and Tara Buck.
  • Acclaimed stage productions include Eric Bogosian's Suburbia at The Skylight Theatre Company, Sam Shepard's True West, John Patrick Shanley's Italian American Reconciliation, and Robert Bolt's A Man for All Seasons alongside John Glover and directed by Howard Deutch.

THE VISION

Michael's work is rooted in the belief that truth, imagination, and courage are the core of every great performance. His mission is to help actors discover the artist within themselves by living truthfully in the work - turning craft into art, one scene at a time.
