Michael Petted: Artist and Educator

A mission to turn craft into art, one scene at a time.

The Educator

Michael Petted has been a dedicated acting teacher since 2009, when he began teaching full-time at the renowned Beverly Hills Playhouse, where he spent ten years shaping the craft of hundreds of actors - ultimately teaching thousands through his classes, workshops, and international programs. The Playhouse is known for its celebrated alumni, including George Clooney, Michelle Pfeiffer, Alec Baldwin, Jorge Garcia, and Monica Barbaro, among many others.

For eight years, Michael also served as the Head Acting Instructor for the Hollywood Immersive program, working with actors from around the world—including Australia, England, and Africa—helping them bridge the gap between training and professional work.

Today, Michael teaches his own scene study and on-camera acting class at The Hudson Theatre in Los Angeles, where his passion for truth, artistry, and practical technique continues to inspire actors at every level - from complete beginners to seasoned professionals.

Michael's students have gone on to work on acclaimed series and films including Yellowstone, The Promised Land (current series lead), And Just Like That, A Big Bold Beautiful Journey, American Horror Story, and Far and Away (Australia), among others. His classes focus on moment-to-moment work, using imagination and lived experience to create authentic, exciting performances - while also emphasizing the realities of a working career. Michael believes great acting comes from living truthfully, creating fearlessly, and turning craft into art one scene at a time.

The Artist Behind the Teacher

As an actor, Michael's work spans television, film, and stage. His screen credits include FX's American Horror Story (opposite Jessica Lange), FOX's New Girl, and the indie feature Mayor Cupcake, alongside Lea Thompson, Judd Nelson, and Zoey Deutch. He also starred in Spade (opposite Jorge Garcia) and A Good Thing (with Tom Bower and Anne Gee Byrd).

He appeared in recurring sketches on NBC's The Late Late Show alongside Thomas Lennon, and most recently in the award-winning short Beautiful Dead Things, featured in over twenty festivals worldwide and now streaming on Amazon Prime.

On stage, Michael has appeared in acclaimed productions including Eric Bogosian's Suburbia at The Skylight Theatre Company, Sam Shepard's True West, John Patrick Shanley's Italian American Reconciliation, and Robert Bolt's A Man for All Seasons alongside John Glover, directed by Howard Deutch.

He earned an Ovation Award nomination for Best Actor for his role in Lone Star (opposite Don Swayze and produced by Patrick Swayze and Doris Roberts) and starred in the Ovation-nominated A Gift from Heaven alongside Beth Grant and Tara Buck.

Michael has been directed by George Clooney, Gene Reynolds, and Howard Deutch, among others. He is also an accomplished musician and songwriter, having performed with icons such as Slash and Bob Dylan, and continues to write, record, and perform his own music.
